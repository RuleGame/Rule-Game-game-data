You are invited to participate in a research study that is being conducted by Jacob Feldman, Ph.D., who is a professor in the Psychology Department at Rutgers University. The U.S. Department of Defense is funding the study.

The purpose of this research is to better understand the mechanisms of human perception and cognition. Approximately 5-20 subjects between the ages of 18 and 25 years old will participate in each condition of this study, and each individual's participation will last less than one hour.

The experiment requires you, the subject, to sit in front of the computer screen, view a series of images, and make responses by pressing keys on the computer keyboard. The task will be described in more detail before the experimental session begins.

Data drawn from your session will be stored with your initials only. Since we are interested in general trends, your data may be pooled with that of others. There are no risks to your health of any kind. You can stop the experiment at any time.

Responses in psychology experiments are sometimes altered if the subject is aware of the hypotheses under consideration. Hence we cannot explain the precise purpose of the experiment until after the session is over. However, afterwards the experimenter will be happy to answer any questions you might have about the procedure or underlying scientific project.

Your participation is entirely voluntary and you may stop at any time. If for any reason you would like to discontinue the experiment, please end the session. If you do decide to terminate the session before it is complete you will be paid or credited for the time you have spent on a prorated basis.

This research is confidential. Confidential means that the research records will include some information about you, such as your gender and age. Although there are no direct benefits to you by your participation in this study, others in the future may benefit from the results of this research.
